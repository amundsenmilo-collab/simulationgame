# Asford Materials Hyperrealism Empire Builder — Game Context

## The Company

**Asford Materials** — a concrete and aggregate producer in Alabama. Founded 1978 by Barry Asford Sr. Operates a batch plant in Sylacauga and a form shop in Hoover. Serves DOT, commercial construction, and specialty markets.

**2026 Starting Position:**
- Revenue: $28M annually
- EBITDA: $4.48M (16% margin)
- Debt: $3.3M
- Cash: $1.2M
- Plant utilization: 87%
- Employees: 42

## The Player

**Connor Asford** — age 25. Inherited the company after his father Barry Jr. died unexpectedly in 2025. No formal business training. Inherits a company in transition: aging equipment, deferred maintenance, union pressure, and a bank relationship that's cordial but watchful.

**Personality:** Quiet. Observant. Doesn't bluff. Asks questions before deciding. Respects the people who built the company but knows things need to change.

## Key NPCs

### Mike Castellano (Plant Manager)
- Age 58. Worked for Barry Sr. and Barry Jr. Knows every machine on the floor.
- Loyal but aging. Wants equity. Wants to know Connor has a plan.
- Trust starts at 75/100 (loyal). Drops if Connor ignores his advice or cuts corners on maintenance.

### Patricia Holt (Bank Relationship Manager)
- Age 52. Manages the $3.3M credit facility. Professional, cautious, by-the-book.
- Trust starts at 60/100 (cooperative). Watches DSCR and cash flow obsessively.
- Will call early if covenants slip. Will not renegotiate easily.

### Mother (Unnamed)
- Age 78. Widow. Owns 10% of the company (Connor owns 90%). Wants stability and legacy.
- Trust starts at 80/100 (loyal). Concerned about Connor's youth but supportive.

### Sister (Unnamed)
- Age 28. Works in the office (accounting/admin). Wants to be involved in decisions.
- Trust starts at 70/100 (cooperative). Will push back if she feels excluded.

### Business Agent (Union)
- Represents the 12 hourly workers. Adversarial. Union election in 45 days (from start of game).
- Trust starts at 40/100 (neutral). Will go lower if Connor resists fair wages.

### Harold Vance (Competitor)
- Runs Forterra Materials, the main competitor. Older, established, aggressive on pricing.
- Trust starts at 50/100 (neutral). Will undercut on bids. Respects strength.

### Darius Cole (Supplier)
- Supplies raw materials (limestone, sand, cement). Transactional relationship.
- Trust starts at 45/100 (neutral). Reliable but will raise prices if he senses weakness.

## Immediate Crises

1. **Union Election (45 days)** — The hourly workers are voting on union representation. If they vote yes, wages and benefits will rise. Connor must decide: fight it, accept it, or negotiate early.

2. **Deferred Maintenance** — The batch plant has $400K in deferred maintenance. The kiln is 18 years old. Equipment failures are becoming more frequent.

3. **Capacity Constraints** — At 87% utilization, the plant is near max capacity. Growth requires either new equipment or a second shift. Both are expensive.

4. **Bank Covenant** — The credit facility requires DSCR > 3.0x and minimum cash of $200K. Current DSCR is 3.2x. Tight.

## The World

**Market:** Concrete is a commodity. Margins are thin. Competition is local and fierce. DOT work is steady but low-margin. Commercial work is higher-margin but cyclical.

**Economy:** 2026 is stable. No recession in sight. Construction is steady. But interest rates are high (7.5%), which makes borrowing expensive.

**Tone:** This is not a feel-good game. The world is indifferent. Decisions have consequences. Some paths lead to growth; others lead to survival; others lead to failure. There are no perfect solutions.

## Narrative Voice

The narrator is objective and unsentimental. No cheerleading. No ensuring victory. The world is indifferent to Connor's youth or good intentions.

Narratives should:
- Ground themselves in specific, sensory detail (smells, sounds, temperatures, textures)
- Show character motivation through dialogue and action, not explanation
- Embed financial reality into the story (margins, covenants, cash flow)
- Reflect the year's character (boom, crisis, stable, etc.)
- Acknowledge relationship dynamics (who trusts Connor, who doesn't)
- Be 2-3 paragraphs, not a list

Example tone:
> "The year started with a backlog Connor had not seen since his grandfather's era. The batch plant smelled of wet cement and diesel. Mike Castellano moved his tools to the new kiln in March, three weeks behind schedule. By June, the margin was 18 percent. Patricia Holt's year-end call was brief: 'See me in January.' Connor signed the year-end statements at 11 PM, alone in the office."

## Game Mechanics (Not Narrative)

Each year, Connor issues directives (e.g., "expand production," "cut costs," "invest in equipment," "negotiate with union"). The financial engine calculates the impact. The narrative engine describes what happened.

Directives affect:
- Revenue (expand +8%, cut -3%, maintain 0%)
- Operating expenses (hire +6%, automate -4%, maintain 0%)
- Debt (borrow for capex, pay down debt)
- Covenant status (DSCR, cash flow)

The narrative should reflect these choices and their consequences.
