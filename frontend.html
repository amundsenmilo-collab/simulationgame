Industry Briefing

About Asford Materials

Know the market before you move.

What We Make

Asford Materials manufactures precast concrete infrastructure products — the unglamorous backbone of American civil construction. Every road drainage system, every utility vault, every municipal manhole is a widget in this industry.

  • RCP Pipe — Reinforced Concrete Pipe, 12–96 inch diameter. Manufactured to ASTM C76. The workhorse product. Storm drainage, culverts, sanitary sewer. DOT-spec.
  • Manholes & Junction Boxes — Precast concrete access structures. Municipal utility work. Repeat customers. Relationship-driven sales.
  • Inlets & End Treatments — Curb inlets, flared end sections, headwalls. Smaller-ticket, higher-margin. Often bundled with pipe orders.

The Precast Concrete Market

$170B global 5.6–6.3% CAGR US = largest market

The global precast concrete market is valued at approximately $170 billion and is growing at a compound annual rate of 5.6–6.3%, driven by infrastructure investment, urbanization, and the shift away from field casting.

The US market is the single largest geography. Federal infrastructure spending — IIJA, state DOT budgets, municipal bond issuance — is the primary demand driver. Precast is not democratized.

Regional producers like Asford compete on delivery radius, relationship depth, and price. Concrete is heavy. Freight economics limit how far a plant can ship. A Birmingham plant serves Alabama, Georgia, Tennessee, Mississippi — not California.

Private Equity in Precast

The precast concrete industry is in the middle of a PE-driven consolidation wave. The thesis is simple: the market is fragmented, demand is infrastructure-linked and stable, and there are operational efficiencies to capture.

KPS Capital Partners has assembled a platform of 202 facilities with $19.5B in AUM. They are not buying companies — they are building a national infrastructure company.

TJC (The Jordan Company) and other mid-market PE firms are running similar strategies at smaller scale. The pattern is consistent: identify a fragmented market with recurring demand, acquire 5–10 regional players, roll them into a single entity, optimize costs, and exit at a multiple.

PE threat is existential.

A PE-backed competitor with 20 plants and a $500M credit facility can price below your cost of capital on a DOT bid and absorb the loss. They are not trying to win that bid — they are building market share. You cannot compete with that.
